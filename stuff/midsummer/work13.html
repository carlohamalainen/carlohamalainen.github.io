Prague Midsummer Combinatorial Workshop XIII

July 30 - August 3, 2007

Only for invited participants.

First announcement

The workshop continues the tradition of Prague Combinatorial Workshops held since 1993. Oriented on problems of all fields of graph theory, combinatorics and discrete geometry, it will continue in the spirit and informal working atmosphere of the previous meetings. The workshop takes place at DIMATIA and the Department of Applied Mathematics of Charles University which is located in a historic building in the center of Old Prague. For the seventh time the workshop is also supported by ITI Charles University (Center for Theoretical Computer Science). A booklet of problems communicated at the workshop will be published in the Department KAM-DIMATIA Series.

The workshop will start on Monday July 30th 2007 at 9am at the department.

All e-mail correspondence concerning the workshop (e.g. accomodation booking) should be directed to mcw2007@kam.mff.cuni.cz.

junior organizers: Martin Pergel, Tomas Valla

senior organizers: Jan Kratochvil, Jaroslav Nesetril

The meeting is going to take place in building at Malonstranske nam. 25.

There will be NO conference fee. The workshop will start on Monday July 30th 2007 at 9am at the department at Malostranske namesti 25 by registration. The conference will continue until lunch at 1PM. This will be the pattern of every day of our meeting and after the session a lunch will be offered in our university restaurant (the basement of the same building). Afternoons are reserved for discussions. The programme will be determined each day for the following day. You are expected to let us know about your intended talk on Monday. The workshop follows an informal routine with enough time for discussions.

There will be several special occasions:

  • Wednesday 1st August, afternoon: ride through the hearth of Prague in a historical tram with guided tour
  • Wednesday 1st August, 19:00: conference dinner
  • All the necessary details will be announced during the workshop.
